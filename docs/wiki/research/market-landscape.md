**Curated:** 2026-06-25 | **Source research:** 2026-05-28
**Consolidates:** Des Moines market landscape, competitive positioning, pricing models (overnight research)

The market intelligence behind BGD's pivot to "I solve business problems with AI." Covers who else is selling AI services in Des Moines, where the white space is, how the work is priced, and what Justin's actual moat looks like.

## The thesis in one line

**Practical, build-capable AI consulting for mid-market Iowa companies — working systems in 30-60 days at $3K-$10K/mo — is an open position nobody in Central Iowa owns yet.**

Iowa's 13.8% business AI adoption rate is among the lowest nationally. That's the opportunity, not the problem: the market is early, competition is thin, and first-mover advantage is real. Demand is already being manufactured (CIRAS has trained 1,400+ professionals) but nobody is fulfilling it for the mid-market.

## Where the demand is (ICPs by industry)

Des Moines metro is dense with $5M-$500M companies that have 20-500 employees, no CTO, known-inefficient manual processes, budget for solutions but not R&D, and a strong preference for someone local they trust.

| Industry | AI readiness | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| **Insurance / financial** (DSM is #1-2 US insurance capital) | Med-High | Carrier agency networks (independent agents) are massively underserved: doc processing, claims intake, compliance reporting. Network effects — land one carrier's agency network, repeat. |
| **Agriculture / agbioscience** (highest opportunity gap) | Low-Med | Dealers/co-ops have near-zero AI adoption. Inventory forecasting, grower marketing, route optimization. Paper-heavy, relationship-driven. Aligns with BrandOS dealer relationships. |
| **Manufacturing** (most urgent need; 6,000+ in Iowa) | Low | Quality inspection, predictive maintenance, order processing. ERP is still priority #1 ahead of AI. **Grant-funded** (see below). |
| **Logistics / transportation** | Med | Route optimization, automated dispatch, fleet predictive maintenance. Already tech-forward. |
| **Healthcare** | Low-Med | Admin automation (scheduling, billing, prior auth). Compliance-heavy. |

Three natural ICPs — manufacturing, agriculture, insurance agencies — align with BGD's existing network and BrandOS dealer relationships.

### Iowa-specific accelerators

- **Grant funding as a sales opener.** Manufacturing 4.0 Technology Investment Program: up to **$75K/business**, 1:1 match. The state pays half; Justin's fee is the other half. No competitor uses this consistently. ("We'll help you apply for the grant AND do the implementation.")
- **In-person trust beats remote.** Iowa owners buy from people they know. A local who shows up and ships beats a remote consultancy every time.
- **The CIRAS pipeline creates demand without fulfillment.** 1,400+ trained professionals now aware of what's possible but don't know who to hire.
- **Other funds for BGD itself:** IEDA Innovation Acceleration Fund (Launch $250K / Propel $500K / Expansion $1M), POCR ($50K loans) — relevant if the AI service line or BrandOS productizes.

## How the work is priced

### National benchmarks (2026)

- **Hourly:** Junior $100-150 · Mid $150-300 · Senior/principal $300-500+ · boutique-senior sweet spot $200-350.
- **Project:** Readiness audit $5K-35K · Pilot/MVP $10K-40K · Production build $25K-150K · Enterprise $100K-500K+.
- **Retainers:** Advisory 5-10 hrs $2K-5K/mo · Standard 10-25 hrs $5K-15K/mo · AI system support (monitoring + tuning) $2K-8K/mo.
- **Market shift:** away from hourly toward **packaged outcomes**. Discovery $1.5-3K → Build $5-15K → Retainer $1-3K/mo is the emerging productized shape.

Reference points: Arsum (one workflow $3-10K + $500-1.5K/mo; case study payback 4.9 mo), AGIX (won't take a project without provable 3-5x first-year return), Fractional AI Officer model ($5-15K/mo, ~50-70% under a full-time CTO).

### BGD's three-tier model

The recommended structure, grounded in the benchmarks above. Land low, expand up. See also the [BrandOS growth strategy](brandos-growth-strategy.md) and the [client opportunity audit](client-opportunity-audit.md) for where to apply it.

| Tier | Price | Target | What they get | Your time | Effective rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — AI Quickstart** | $750/mo + $2,500 setup | 10-50 emp, owner drowning in manual work | Readiness audit, 2-3 quick-win automations, monthly call, monitoring, ≤5 hrs/mo | 8-12 hr setup, 3-5 hr/mo | ~$130-250/hr |
| **2 — AI Operations Partner** (workhorse) | $2,500/mo + $5-10K setup | 50-200 emp, multi-dept manual handoffs | Custom builds (1-2 workflows/mo), system integrations, dashboards, process redesign, ≤15 hrs/mo, bi-weekly call | 20-40 hr setup, 10-15 hr/mo | ~$150-250/hr |
| **3 — Fractional AI Officer** | $5,000/mo (no setup) | 100-500 emp, C-suite needs AI lead | Strategic role, ~25-30 hrs/mo capacity, custom agents, quarterly roadmap, same-day priority, monthly training | 20-30 hr/mo | ~$150-240/hr |

**ROI framing per tier:** Tier 1 — "recover ~$975/mo of admin time, pays for itself in month one." Tier 2 — "you lose 2 deals/mo to slow lead response; that's $10K/mo." Tier 3 — "a full-time AI hire is $200K+; you get the same for $60K/yr, no headcount lock-in."

**Value-based option** for larger engagements: base retainer (~$2,500/mo) + 15% of documented annual savings. Aligns incentives, raises revenue per client.

### Land-and-expand (MindStudio ladder)

Don't pitch retainers cold. Climb:
- **Rung 0 — door opener ($500-2,500):** Readiness audit, process-mapping session, or one quick-win build. **Always end with a findings presentation:** what you found, what you'd build, prioritized roadmap, expected ROI. Present three ideas ranked by impact/ease; make one an obvious quick win. Rung 1 closes in the room.
- **Rung 1 — first paid project ($3-7.5K):** Build one automation, document ROI, make the client a hero internally.
- **Rung 2 — second project + adjacent win ($5-10K):** Expand to another department.
- **Rung 3 — retainer pitch:** After 3-4 successes, the monthly arrangement is the natural next step.

**Stickiness:** monitoring/maintenance (models drift), quarterly optimization, institutional knowledge (high switching cost), and a continuous drip of small monthly quick wins.

**Path to $10K/mo MRR (Scenario A — volume):** 6× Tier 1 ($4,500) + 2× Tier 2 ($5,000) + amortized project revenue ≈ $10,000/mo at ~50-60 hrs/mo — sustainable alongside 2RM + BGD web work. Setup revenue collected along the way: $25-40K.

## Justin's moat

No single piece is unique. The moat is the **stack** — almost nobody in the Des Moines mid-market has all of it at once:

1. **Production-tested technical depth.** 10+ years shipping real software (custom Craft modules, data importers, geocoding, multi-tenant platforms, commerce, DevOps that survives Iowa State Fair traffic spikes). A decade of receipts behind "I can build this."
2. **AI proficiency beyond prompting.** Built his own AI operating system, automated triage, context hydration across a dozen clients. Can assess what AI will actually do for a specific problem.
3. **Existing relationships with the exact buyer profile.** Kirk Financial (Wild Rose, Thermal Kitchen), Terraplex + dealer network, Hatch DSM (Iowa State Fair), NPS Media, IowaEverywhere — 5-10 year trust, not cold leads.
4. **A working product as proof.** BrandOS.

**vs. big firms:** 10-50x cheaper, builds (not slide decks), available, already knows the client's systems. Weakness: one person, can't staff parallel workstreams — but the mid-market rarely needs that.

**vs. SaaS tools they buy direct:** off-the-shelf solves generic problems; Justin solves *their* problem, wires the integrations, and tunes it over time.

### The language that works

- "I build the thing, not just the plan for the thing."
- "You'll talk to me, not a PM who talks to a developer who talks to a junior."
- "I'm not selling you AI. I'm selling you your time back."
- "Every solution I build for one client becomes something I can adapt for the next."

In Justin's register: *"I figure out what's eating your time and I build something to fix it. If AI helps, I use AI. If it doesn't, I don't."*

Avoid: "AI-powered digital transformation," "cutting-edge machine learning," "full-stack AI solutions" — consultant-speak that trips BS detectors.

## Honest risks

- **Scope creep** — fixed-scope Rung-0 engagements and clear retainer boundaries.
- **Expectations outpacing reality** — under-promise, demo working systems not capabilities.
- **AI hype-cycle backlash** — lead with outcomes ("time back"), not buzzwords.
- **Bandwidth** — Justin is one person across 2RM + BGD web + this. The volume-play scenario is the sustainable one.
- **Sales dependency on Jon** — diversify the demand channel; never gate the line on one person. (See [decisions log] and partnership notes.)
- **Maintenance burden** — running automations is ongoing work; price the retainer to cover drift/tuning.
