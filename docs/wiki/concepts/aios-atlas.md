# The AIOS Atlas — Cross-Project Rollup Concept

The Atlas is the **breadth layer** for AIOS-level situational awareness: a single curated-prose page answering "where does everything stand across all projects," generated by reading each project wiki. It complements — does not replace — the two other cross-project surfaces:

- **The ctx / knowledge-graph cache** answers "find every mention of X" and relationship-chain queries. The Atlas answers "where does everything stand."
- **`state/<slug>.md`** holds the thin, dashboard-driven per-project delta at the operational layer.

Read the Atlas for the lay of the land, then `/load <slug>` (or the project's own wiki) for depth.

## Why it exists

Flat-file routing and keyword search can't synthesize a portfolio-wide picture. The Atlas is curated prose precisely so it can answer general "how's everything going" questions and, more importantly, surface **cross-project flags** — collisions and risks no single project's wiki can see because they span projects.

## The high-value output: cross-project flags

The flags are the reason to maintain it. From the 2026-06-01 POC pass, three patterns the per-project wikis individually missed:

- **Capacity collisions.** Multiple builds + launches stacking in one window (e.g. June 2026: Thermal Kitchen build + launch, ToneQuest launch, Mr Gym build, and a BrandOS deadline window all overlapping). Several projects independently flag Justin's Q2 load as a risk — the Atlas is where that adds up.
- **Pre-launch security/delivery risk.** A launch-blocking issue on one project (e.g. ToneQuest paywalled HTML shipped client-side before a hard launch date) becomes the highest-urgency item across the whole portfolio.
- **Ventures paused or at risk.** A venture quietly hollowing out (e.g. Deploy Answers' own products paused to feed BrandOS, risking an empty shell) only reads as a risk when you look across the portfolio.

## How it would stay current

The intended maintenance model: a commit hook regenerates the *changed* project's block (fan out one reader for that project) and rewrites the file. The ctx cache handles cross-project mention search; the Atlas handles cross-project status. Use both.

## Status

A sample / POC, first generated 2026-06-01. The concept is validated — the value is in the synthesized flags, not the per-project blocks (those duplicate `state/` and each project wiki). If made live, the per-project sections should stay thin and the flags section should carry the weight.
