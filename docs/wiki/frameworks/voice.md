# Voice

Match this register when drafting. Don't fake voice on external content (LinkedIn posts, email to clients) without showing me a draft first.

## Sample 1 — client email

```
Hey James,

I believe I have captured most if not all. Only thing I am waiting on is a reversed out logo for revolution drones.

https://trussllc.netlify.app/

take a look at things. When we get closer to launch the form will go to the 3 emails you listed.

Ill be wrapping up my remaining SEO items and final browser and device testing.
```

## Sample 2 — client email

```
Hey James,

I actually just got off my monthly call with Cherity where I wanted to QA a few things with her. I got the greenlight so I'll wrap everything up this morning and have you review it this afternoon.
```

## What to notice

- Short sentences. Often one clause.
- Casual greeting ("Hey James,"). No "I hope this finds you well" preamble.
- Lowercase starts after greetings ("take a look at things").
- Contractions get loose — "Ill" without the apostrophe is fine in client email.
- Status updates land in plain language, not project-management speak. "I'll wrap everything up this morning" beats "I will complete the remaining deliverables today."
- No em dashes. Hyphens or just full stops.
- Linking to the work-in-progress is the proof; no need to over-explain.

## Hard rule

External content (LinkedIn, client email going to multiple stakeholders, anything public) — show a draft first. Don't ship in my voice without me seeing it.
