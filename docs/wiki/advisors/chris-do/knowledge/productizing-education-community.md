# Productizing Education and Community -- The Futur's Business Model

**URL:** https://www.outseta.com/posts/how-the-futur-built-a-1m-community
**Published:** 2023
**Type:** Case study -- How The Futur Built a $1M+ Community (Outseta)
**Also draws from:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y3S-y-mluZA (Chris Do agency growth interview), https://podcasts.apple.com/nz/podcast/stop-doing-everything-w-mo-ismail-ep-426/id1209219220 (Five Ones framework)

## Key Frameworks and Patterns

### The Origin Story: Agency to Education Pivot

Chris Do and Jose Caballer met as students at ArtCenter in Los Angeles. Both built successful agency careers -- Do ran Blind, a creative agency with clients like Sony, Audi, and EA Sports, for over 15 years.

Caballer's pitch to make YouTube videos together: "Why continue teaching in a classroom where you can only impact 15 people at a time, when YouTube can turn the entire planet into your classroom?"

Do launched The Futur and reoriented from agency work to education. The pivot was driven by a realization: "After realizing he didn't need to work another day for financial reasons, Chris made a bold pivot: focusing his life's work on impact over income."

### The Self-Funding Content Flywheel

The Futur's business model is a flywheel with a counterintuitive core principle:

**"All of our paid products exist to allow us to invest more money in producing our free YouTube content. Our goal is to fund our free education by helping business people make money."** -- Ben Burns, COO

The product tiers:

1. **Free YouTube content** (2M+ subscribers) -- builds audience, passive learning. Top-of-funnel. This is the primary product, not the paid offerings.
2. **Courses** ($149-$199) -- condensed, structured versions of YouTube content. Brand Strategy Fundamentals, Color for Creatives, Painless Pricing. "Active learning" tier.
3. **Resources/Templates** -- the best sellers by quantity. Practical, actionable tools (e.g., a proposal template used to close over $10M in business).
4. **Pro Group Community** ($449/quarter or $1,495/annually) -- the highest-touch tier. Circle-based community with coaching, Discord channels, and peer support.

Product philosophy: "Our YouTube content builds our audience and is more passive learning. The courses and community are for more active learners. The resources are things you can immediately put into action yourself."

### Community Monetization Insights

The Pro Group community alone generates $1M+ annual revenue from 900+ members. Key operational decisions:

- **Quarterly billing over monthly.** "A monthly commitment is not enough to see results, and a year is often too much of a financial commitment to make up front. But if we can get a member to commit for 3 months, they usually stay for 6 or 9 or 12 months." Result: 3x improvement in revenue retention after switching from monthly to quarterly.
- **Migration from Facebook Groups + Slack to Circle.** Combined the best of both platforms with proper SSO so members log in once for both website and community.
- **Help desk over Zendesk.** Direct support line for community members increases perceived value.
- **Community members become team members.** Marcelo Russo joined as a Pro Group member, became the most active participant, volunteered to help with Webflow projects, was put on retainer, and eventually became the agency of record (Fri3nds Agency) managing The Futur's 120-page website and community infrastructure.

### The Five Ones Framework (Narrowing Focus)

From a later podcast episode, Do advocates radical focus for creative entrepreneurs:

- **One target market.** Not three or four "ideal clients." One.
- **One offering.** Not a menu of services. One core thing you deliver.
- **One channel.** Not spread across every platform. Pick one and dominate it.
- **One content format.** Not video + blog + podcast + newsletter. One format, mastered.
- **One year.** Commit to the above for a full year before evaluating.

The metaphor: narrowing focus creates the pressure needed to "punch through industry noise." A diluted personal brand with five offerings for four markets is a hose with no nozzle -- lots of water, no pressure.

This requires "burying your old professional identity." If you used to be a generalist designer, that identity must die for the specialist brand strategist to live.

### The Business Bootcamp Model

Do productized his 25 years of running two businesses (Blind + The Futur) into an 8-week cohort:

- Price: $5,000
- Target: creative professionals scaling from $100K to $250K+ or into the millions
- Covers: talking to clients, presenting creative, running a business
- Not just theory -- application with real-world feedback

The education business aspiration: "The Netflix of business design education." Revenue from paid products funds making content more accessible and farther-reaching. Vision includes hybrid learning: some online, some in-person.

## Positions and Opinions

- Free content is the primary product, not a marketing expense. Paid products fund the free content, not the other way around. This inverts the typical creator economy model.
- Quarterly billing is the sweet spot for community retention. Monthly is too short for members to see results. Annual is too big a commitment upfront.
- Community members are the best talent pipeline. The most engaged members understand your mission and can become team members organically.
- Radical focus (Five Ones) beats diversification for solo/small operators. Narrowing creates the pressure to break through noise.
- "Burying" your old professional identity is required for a pivot to take hold. You cannot be both generalist and specialist. The old identity must die.
- Education at scale is the higher-leverage play over agency services. One person teaching millions creates more impact than one person serving dozens of clients.
- Products at every price point, including free, maximizes reach while supporting sustainability.

## How This Applies as a Decision Lens

The Futur's flywheel model is a long-term template for BGD's evolution. Currently Justin sells services (agency model). The next move is productized services (AI services tiers). But the endgame, if Justin follows Do's trajectory, could be education -- teaching mid-market companies how to leverage AI, funded by a community of operators. The Five Ones framework is immediately relevant: Justin is at risk of the diluted brand problem (Craft CMS + BrandOS + AI services + Deploy Answers). Pick one target market, one offering, one channel, and commit for a year. The quarterly billing insight applies directly to any recurring BGD service tier. The community-to-team pipeline is a pattern to watch: BGD's best future hires may come from people who first engage as clients or community members, not from job postings.
