## Key Frameworks and Patterns

### Career Arc: Goldman Sachs to AI Empire

Full name: Nate Herkelman. Born April 2002. University of Iowa, Tippie College of Business, double major in Business Analytics and Marketing. Business Student Ambassador. Moved to Salt Lake City for Goldman Sachs as Business Intelligence Analyst. Built dashboards and process automations using Alteryx and modern BI stacks. Left Goldman Sachs in November 2024 to go full-time on AI automation.

Key insight from Goldman: saw how much leverage automation could create, and how limited traditional rule-based systems were compared to what AI could unlock.

### TrueHorizon AI: Enterprise AI Services at Scale

Co-founded TrueHorizon AI in January 2025. Focus: helping enterprise businesses implement intelligent systems to scale operations. Scaled to over $100,000/month revenue in under a year. Worked with major brands including Topgolf and BarkBox.

Exited TrueHorizon AI in December 2025 (11-month run). Brought the hands-on experience of building and scaling an AI services business back to educational content and community.

Why this matters: Nate's advice is grounded in running a real agency that hit $100K+/month with enterprise clients, not just teaching theory. He built it, scaled it, and chose to exit -- which gives him perspective on both the upside and the operational burden of agency work.

### Three Revenue Streams Model

Nate's own business demonstrates the model he teaches:
1. **Free content (YouTube).** Top-of-funnel. Weekly tutorials, deep dives. Drives awareness and trust.
2. **Community and courses (Skool).** Mid-funnel (free community) and bottom-funnel (paid $99/mo). Structured curriculum, templates, support network.
3. **AI partner and strategy work (Uppit AI).** High-ticket. Select long-term engagements. Roadmap design, not one-off builds.

This three-layer stack (content -> community -> services) is itself a blueprint for how to structure an AI services business.

## Positions and Opinions

- Build the agency, learn from it, then decide if you want to keep it or pivot to education/products. The experience of running a real agency is irreplaceable.
- Enterprise clients (Topgolf, BarkBox scale) validate AI services as a real business, not a freelance side hustle.
- Strategic AI partner model beats project-based work. Design roadmaps, not one-offs.
- The three-layer stack (content -> community -> services) creates compounding distribution that no single revenue stream can match.
- Exiting an agency you scaled to $100K/month is a valid move. Good operators know when to build AND when to pivot.
- Competitive building events (Agentic Arena) validate practical skill in a way that tutorials and courses cannot.
