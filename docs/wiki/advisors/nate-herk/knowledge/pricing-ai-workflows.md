# How to Price AI Workflows (Without Losing Clients)

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wIcw0T9NhZM
**Published:** 2025-11-30
**Type:** YouTube video

## Key Frameworks and Patterns

### The Mindset Shift
Most beginners price based on time/effort. Businesses don't pay for your time. They pay for outcomes. When you build an AI workflow, you're doing at least one of three things: saving money, saving time, or reducing human error. Your price needs to be tied to the return on their investment.

### Value-Based Pricing
- Charge based on business impact, not hours spent building.
- Anchor price around time saved, money saved, or efficiency gain.
- Same system, two different businesses, completely different prices. "If you tried to sell me water after running 5 miles in the desert, I'd pay more than someone in an air-conditioned office."
- Value is not universal. Price based on what the solution is worth to THAT business.

### The 10x Rule of Thumb
The client should see a 10x return on investment in the first year. Example: a workflow saving $12,000/year in employee time should be priced around $1,200 (10% of annual savings). Walk the client through the math step by step -- builds instant trust.

### Don't Forget Opportunity Cost
Freeing up an hour of an employee's time doesn't just save the business money. It enables that employee to shift into higher-value work -- revenue-generating opportunities.

### Three Pricing Models (progression)

1. **Value-Based Pricing (start here).** One-off projects tied to ROI. Easiest way to get your foot in the door. The math is simple and builds trust.

2. **Monthly Retainers (after trust is built).** Predictable monthly fee for ongoing access + defined service level. Range: $1,500-$15,000+/month. 3, 6, or 12 month terms. One $15K/month client > five $3K/month clients (less chaos, real relationship).

3. **Performance/Revenue-Share (advanced).** Tie pricing directly to measurable outcomes.

### Presentation Framework
- Never start with the price. Start with the transformation.
- Get the client imagining what the business looks like once the system is live.
- Explain everything included: setup, hosting, testing, optimization, documentation, training, maintenance.
- Walk through proposed solution before ever saying a number.

### Real Example
An automation saving a client $38,000/year. Priced at 15% of annualized savings = $5,500. Walked through the solution first (how sales agents work, what it automates, testing, what's needed from them). By the time the price came up, the client was already bought into the outcome.

### Scope Protection
When clients ask for bigger features during QA, protect the scope. Tell them which requests fit v1 and which go to a backlog for a future phase. This alone saves from doing tons of unpaid work.

## Positions and Opinions

- Pricing by hours spent is a beginner mistake. Charge for outcomes, not inputs.
- Walk clients through the ROI math step by step. Transparency builds trust faster than discounting.
- Retainers are the end goal -- stable income and long-term partnership instead of chasing one-off builds.
- You'd rather work with one high-value client than five cheaper ones at the same total revenue.
- Scope creep is the biggest threat to profitability. Define v1 clearly, backlog everything else.
- Don't overpromise results. If the workflow only automates half the process, factor in that half.
