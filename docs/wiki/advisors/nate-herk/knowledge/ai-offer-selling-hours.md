# The AI Offer You Can Sell Tomorrow Morning

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pi-m8R068r4
**Published:** 2026-05-22
**Type:** YouTube video

## Key Frameworks and Patterns

### The Ladder of AI Offers (from bottom to top)

- **Rung 0: Selling Hours.** $100-$500/session. One-on-one consulting helping business owners set up their AI operating system or get comfortable with tools like Claude Code. This is where to START.
- **Rung 1: Paid Audit.** $500-$2,500. Go in for 1-2+ hours, map their workflows, find what's automatable, put together a proposal for the first real build.
- **Rung 2: First Project.** Building the AI operating system or first automation. The actual deliverable.
- **Rung 3+: Retainers.** $5,000-$10,000+/month. Long-term partnerships. The end goal.

### Why Start at Rung 0
Most people are told to start by pitching audits that turn into projects and retainers. That's directionally right but it's rung 3 or 4. Nobody talks about rung 0. That's why people sit frozen -- they can't picture themselves doing the higher rungs yet. Starting by selling hours dissolves imposter syndrome.

### The AI Operating System as Product
The thing you're selling in the hours is helping business owners set up their own AIOS. It captures their business data, subject matter expertise, runs their workflows, helps them operate day-to-day. The business stops being bottlenecked by the owner because the system has all their data and context.

### The Pitch Framework
"I want to help you set up your AI operating system. We're going to connect your business data, extract your subject matter expertise into this AI system so that your business isn't bottlenecked by you anymore and you can focus on strategy. Working on your business rather than in it. Reduces key man risk if you ever want to sell."

### The Key Reframe
The business owner is the only person who can build the right system -- they have the domain expertise, they know the workflows, customers, and IP. Your job is to make their existing expertise dangerous with the tool. You're selling leverage. You're transferring tool fluency to where domain fluency already lives.

### Session Flow
1. Gather background data
2. Make them tool-fluent at a high level (manage overwhelm)
3. Connect data sources (explain MCP/APIs simply)
4. Onboard and extract context into the system
5. It breaks at a natural point after about an hour
6. Schedule next session to keep momentum

### Landing First Clients (7 Steps, layered)
1. Teach your friends first -- just text 3 people and offer to show them Claude Code
2. Build confidence through reps
3. Move to people in your network who own businesses
4. Each session builds the muscle memory and testimonials
5. Progress up the ladder naturally

### Optimizing for the Long Game
You're optimizing for the cash 6 months from now, not the revenue today. All of it starts with reps and experience up front.

## Positions and Opinions

- Imposter syndrome freezes more people than lack of skill. The cure is starting at rung 0, not trying to skip to rung 3.
- The hour is NOT about showing off AI knowledge. It's about managing overwhelm. Every business owner walks in excited but completely overwhelmed.
- Your job is to keep overwhelm low enough that they want to book another session.
- Every consultant now has a tangible product to sell: the AI operating system setup.
- Don't try to build the system FOR them. They are the only ones who can build the right one because they have the domain expertise.
- Start with friends and network. Don't try to cold outreach before you have reps.

This maps directly to how BGD could structure its AI services entry point.
