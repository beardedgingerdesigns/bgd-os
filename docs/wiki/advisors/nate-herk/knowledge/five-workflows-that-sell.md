# The Five Workflows That Actually Sell in 2026

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y3PcRp5RFzk
**Published:** 2026-03-30
**Type:** YouTube video (210K+ views)

## Key Frameworks and Patterns

### The Core Insight
Businesses don't want fancy AI. They want five types of simple, boring workflows that save time, save money, or remove mistakes. The same five patterns appear across every industry -- coaches, real estate, dentists, HVAC, e-commerce.

### The Five Workflows

1. **Speed to Lead.** Respond to new leads within 5 minutes (10x more likely to convert vs 30 minutes later). Average business takes 47 hours. This is the single easiest automation to sell to any service-based business.

2. **Document Processing.** Moving data from documents into systems. Example: accounting firm spending 50 hours/week moving PDF data to spreadsheets ($78K/year). Many of the most valuable document processing workflows are purely rule-based without any AI -- completely deterministic and maintenance-free.

3. **Follow-Up Sequences.** New leads that need multiple touchpoints to convert. 80% of sales require at least 5 follow-ups, but most salespeople stop after 1-2 attempts. Different from reactivation -- this is about leads who just came in.

4. **Database Reactivation.** Forgotten leads from 6+ months ago. Average ROI of 1,200% in the first 60 days. Revenue that's already been paid for via previous marketing spend.

5. **Internal Reporting.** Compiling data across different systems. Fixes the visibility gap that slows every decision.

### The Clogged Pipe Framework
Think of a business as a pipe with water (money) flowing through it. A clog near the beginning makes adding more water (ads, salespeople) pointless. Find and fix the clog first. The five workflows map directly to the five most common clogs:
- Speed to lead -> response gap
- Document processing -> operational bottleneck
- Follow-up sequences -> leaky pipeline
- Database reactivation -> forgotten revenue
- Internal reporting -> visibility gap slowing decisions

### The Diagnostic Question
"If 500 new clients showed up at your business tomorrow, what would break first?" Forces the owner to walk through their entire operation -- every answer reveals a bottleneck and a potential deal.

### Two Paths to Sell
- **Path 1: Niche Specialist.** Pick one workflow, become the expert. Better case studies, stronger positioning, higher prices. "A good steakhouse doesn't need hot dogs."
- **Path 2: Generalist Consultant.** Understand all five. Your job is to identify the actual bottleneck, not pitch a pre-made solution.

### How to Position the Sale
Sell outcomes, not automations. Not "I'll build an n8n workflow." Instead: "I can save you 10 hours a week and cut your admin error rate." The positioning is what people pay for.

## Positions and Opinions

- Most people online build fancy AI solutions, but businesses don't actually want that. They want boring, simple, reliable.
- The same problems appear across every industry. Different industries, same bottlenecks.
- Many high-value automations require zero AI -- purely deterministic rule-based workflows are often the most valuable.
- Don't try to learn all five at once. Pick one, learn it inside and out, build a simple version, show a business owner a demo.
- Your job as a consultant is to find the actual constraint, not what the business owner thinks the problem is.

## How This Applies as a Decision Lens

When evaluating what AI services to offer or what automations to build for clients, filter through these five categories first. They represent the highest-ROI, most repeatable automation opportunities. The clogged pipe framework is especially useful for client discovery -- start with the constraint, not the solution. Also useful for BGD's pivot: which of these five workflows maps to each client's actual pain point?
