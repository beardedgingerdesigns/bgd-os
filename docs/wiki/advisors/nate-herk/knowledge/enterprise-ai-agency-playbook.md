# The Playbook for Enterprise AI Services and Agency Building

**URL:** YouTube (@nateherk) -- "The Playbook for a $100M AI Agency" (with Devin Karns)
**Published:** 2026 (recent)
**Type:** YouTube interview + supplementary article (The Startup Series)

## Key Frameworks and Patterns

### The Market Shift
- AI development value is collapsing. AI readiness services are becoming commoditized.
- The real opportunity is designing scalable, AI-native operations for mid-market to enterprise clients with clear ROI and repeatable frameworks.
- AI will permeate every vertical. Most current "AI agency" plays are doomed unless you package, price, and scale strategically.

### Mid-Market Sweet Spot
- $10M-$250M revenue companies provide the best mix of deal size, repeatability, and procurement discipline.
- Big enough to have real problems and budget, small enough to not have 18-month procurement cycles.

### From Builder to Partner
- The path: solo practitioner -> AI consultant -> AI partner -> exit-ready firm.
- A repeatable framework and clear differentiation ("AI-native operating system") are essential to command high-value contracts.
- Two most important sales accelerants: (1) upfront discovery workshops to align AI strategy with business goals, (2) a written blueprint that defines ROI and required capabilities.
- Having a strong framework allows you to grow beyond single projects into ongoing partnerships.

### The AI-Native Org Thesis
"A few directors and AI systems can produce the same output as a large team." The big opportunity is building an AI-native organization, not just building AI tools for others.

### Pricing at Scale
- Price should align with value delivered (ROI-based or performance-based), not time-and-materials.
- As model costs fall, margins on time-and-materials collapse. Value-based pricing maintains margins.
- Once ARR hits multi-million levels, equity buyers value enterprise capability and repeatable ROI more than services depth.

### Nate's Own Journey (True Horizon AI)
- First project: personalized outreach writer, $1,650. Client just watched a YouTube video and reached out.
- Progression: outreach writer ($1,650) -> sales quoting agent ($4,000) -> Slack assistant ($6,000) -> AI concierge ($12,000).
- $23,000 from four projects. Each used straightforward components: workflow builder, LLM API, vector store.
- Clients didn't care what was running underneath. They cared that it worked and saved time.
- Pricing evolved from guesswork to value-based: estimate hours saved/week x hourly rate x annual multiplier. Show the client the annual savings and the build price feels small.
- Grew into True Horizon AI, scaled to $100K+/month, working with Topgolf, BarkBox, and other enterprise brands.

### Operational Maturity
- Standard operating procedures, QA stages, and account management roles create professional experience.
- Every project: internal testing week + client testing week before launch.
- Capture baseline data to show impact. Creates case studies for future prospects.
- The tension between being the builder and being the salesperson is real. Reputation and process matter as much as technical prowess.

### Five Lessons Emphasized
1. Distribution matters as much as building.
2. Packaging services properly determines pricing power.
3. Accountability in client engagements builds trust.
4. Discovery workshops and written blueprints close deals.
5. Align incentives with clients for large engagements (revenue share, performance bonuses).

## Positions and Opinions

- Most "AI agency" plays will die. The winners package, price, and scale with repeatable frameworks.
- Mid-market ($10M-$250M) is the sweet spot. Not too small (no budget), not too big (endless procurement).
- Building is table stakes. Distribution, packaging, and sales process are the actual differentiators.
- You don't need to be a machine learning researcher. You need to understand a problem deeply and build something that solves it cleanly.
- The best opportunities are in the boring corners of business -- admin, customer support, repetitive messaging.
- Start small, charge for value, package your work like a product. Four solid clients can prove the model.
- Simple, accessible tools beat fancy infrastructure. Keep development quick and margins high.
