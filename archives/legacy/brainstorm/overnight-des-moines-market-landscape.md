## 2. The Gap

### What Exists

| Segment | Example | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Enterprise AI strategy | Opinosis Analytics | Too expensive, too corporate for mid-market |
| Enterprise IT + AI bolt-on | Zirous, Source Allies | Team-based, 6-figure engagements, slow |
| Marketing agency + "AI" | Blue Frog, Tech Stack | Configuring vendor tools, not building |
| Template chatbot resellers | Iowa AI Consulting, Tech Stack Digital | No custom work, no process depth |
| Offshore SEO plays | DataRoot, Shadhin, AI Superior | No local presence |
| Academic/subsidized | ISU CIRAS | Creates awareness, doesn't do the build |

### What's Missing

**A technical problem-solver who can actually build custom AI automations for mid-market Iowa businesses ($5M-$100M) at accessible price points.**

Specifically:
1. **Someone who understands the business first, then applies AI.** The current market is either "here's our AI product, figure out where it fits" or "let's do a 6-month strategy engagement before we build anything."
2. **Someone who can go from conversation to working prototype in weeks, not months.** Mid-market companies don't have the patience or budget for discovery phases that cost $50K before anything is built.
3. **Someone local who shows up.** Every serious local player (Zirous, Source Allies) is selling to enterprises. The $20M manufacturer in Pella or the $50M ag distributor in Ames has no one to call.
4. **Someone who can bridge the ERP/data gap.** Iowa manufacturers' #1 blocker is fragmented data and incomplete ERP implementations. An AI consultant who can work with messy data (not just clean enterprise datasets) has a massive advantage.
5. **Someone who speaks business owner, not data scientist.** The Opinosis approach (PhD-led, framework-heavy) alienates the exact people who need help most.

### BGD's Positioning Opportunity

**"We solve expensive business problems with AI. If you can describe the pain, we can automate it."**

The gap is specifically: practical, build-capable AI consulting for mid-market Iowa companies that delivers working systems (not decks) within 30-60 days at $3K-$10K/month price points. Nobody is doing this in Central Iowa right now.

## 3. Des Moines Business Landscape

### Key Industries and AI Opportunity

**Insurance and Financial Services** (HIGHEST CONCENTRATION)
- Des Moines is the #1 or #2 insurance capital in the US
- Major employers: Principal Financial Group, Wellmark Blue Cross Blue Shield, EMC Insurance, Nationwide (large ops), FBL Financial, Farmers Mutual Hail, CUNA Mutual, Grinnell Mutual
- AI adoption: moderate internally at the big carriers, but their agency networks (independent agents, brokers) are massively underserved
- Opportunity: AI automation for independent insurance agencies (document processing, claims intake, customer communications, compliance reporting)
- **AI readiness: Medium-High (data-rich, process-heavy, but regulated)**

**Agriculture and Agbioscience** (HIGHEST OPPORTUNITY GAP)
- Iowa is #1 in corn, #2 in soybeans, massive livestock operations
- Key companies: Corteva Agriscience (Johnston), DuPont (legacy), dozens of ag equipment dealers, co-ops, input suppliers
- Current AI state: producers are in "learning phase" on data management; precision ag is growing but fragmented; co-ops and dealers have almost zero AI adoption
- Opportunity: dealer/distributor operations automation, inventory forecasting, precision marketing to growers, route optimization for delivery fleets
- **AI readiness: Low-Medium (paper-heavy, relationship-driven, massive automation potential)**

**Manufacturing** (MOST URGENT NEED)
- Iowa has 6,000+ manufacturers; advanced manufacturing is a key DSM industry
- Key companies: Vermeer (Pella, 3,200 employees), Pella Corporation, Ruan (logistics), John Deere (Ankeny ops), EFCO, Accumold
- Current AI state: Iowa has a 13.8% business AI adoption rate (among lowest nationally). Manufacturers cite lack of understanding, cost concerns, workforce fears. ERP implementation is still the #1 priority before AI. Fragmented data, limited in-house expertise, legacy systems are the top blockers.
- 39 manufacturers received Manufacturing 4.0 grants in FY2026 ($1.98M total, avg ~$50K each)
- Opportunity: quality inspection automation, predictive maintenance, production scheduling optimization, document/order processing automation
- **AI readiness: Low (but grant funding available, CIRAS creating awareness)**

**Logistics and Transportation**
- Key companies: Ruan Transportation, Heartland Express, CRST International, Anderson Trucking
- Opportunity: route optimization, fleet management AI, automated dispatch, predictive maintenance for vehicles
- **AI readiness: Medium (already tech-forward for tracking/GPS, ready for AI layer)**

**Healthcare**
- Key employers: UnityPoint Health, MercyOne, Broadlawns
- Heavily regulated, slower to adopt
- Opportunity: administrative automation (scheduling, billing, prior auth), patient communication
- **AI readiness: Low-Medium (compliance-heavy, but massive admin waste)**

**Media and Publishing**
- Meredith/Dotdash Meredith (now IAC) has large Des Moines operations
- Business Record, Corridor Business Journal
- **AI readiness: Medium-High (already digital, content-adjacent)**

### Mid-Market Sweet Spot

The Des Moines metro has a dense concentration of companies in the $5M-$500M range across manufacturing, agriculture services, insurance agencies, and professional services. These companies typically have:
- 20-500 employees
- No CTO or dedicated technology leader
- Manual processes they know are inefficient but haven't prioritized
- Budget for solutions but not for R&D
- A preference for working with someone local they can trust

This is exactly the segment that's too small for Zirous/Source Allies and too sophisticated for chatbot resellers.

### Iowa-Specific Market Dynamics

1. **Iowa's 13.8% AI adoption rate is among the nation's lowest.** This is a feature, not a bug for Justin. It means the market is early, competition is thin, and first-mover advantage is real.

2. **"In-person trust" matters more in Iowa than in coastal markets.** Iowa business owners buy from people they know. A local Des Moines-based consultant who shows up, speaks plainly, and delivers working systems will beat a remote consultancy every time.

3. **The insurance ecosystem creates network effects.** Des Moines insurance companies have hundreds of agency relationships across Iowa. Land one carrier's agency network and you have a repeatable engagement model.

4. **Manufacturing grant funding creates a built-in sales accelerator.** "The state will pay for half of this" is a conversation opener that no competitor is using consistently.

5. **The CIRAS/ISU pipeline is creating demand without fulfillment.** 1,400+ professionals have attended AI training. They're now aware of what's possible but don't know who to hire to build it. Justin can be the answer to that question.

- Senior consultants: $1,500-$3,000/day
- Domain specialists command 20-40% premium over generalists

**Project-Based Fees**
| Scope | Range |
|---|---|
| AI readiness assessment / audit | $5,000-$35,000 |
| Pilot / MVP / proof of concept | $10,000-$40,000 |
| Production AI build (medium) | $25,000-$150,000 |
| Enterprise-scale deployment | $100,000-$500,000+ |

**Monthly Retainers**
| Tier | Hours/Month | Rate Range |
|---|---|---|
| Advisory / essential | 5-10 hrs | $2,000-$5,000/mo |
| Standard support | 10-25 hrs | $5,000-$15,000/mo |
| Comprehensive / enterprise | 25+ hrs | $15,000-$50,000/mo |
| AI system support (monitoring + tuning) | Varies | $2,000-$8,000/mo |

### Productized AI Service Tiers (Emerging 2026 Model)

The market is shifting from hourly billing to packaged outcomes. Here's what leading AI agencies are pricing:

**Discovery Tier:** $1,500-$3,000
- Strategy + roadmap
- Process audit
- AI opportunity identification

**Build Tier:** $5,000-$15,000
- 1-2 AI agents deployed
- Custom automation workflow
- Integration with existing systems

**Retainer Tier:** $1,000-$3,000/month
- Ongoing tuning and updates
- Performance reporting
- Priority support

### AI Automation Specifically

**Setup/Build Projects:**
- Basic automation (email triggers, chatbots): $2,500-$15,000
- Mid-complexity (multi-system integration, custom logic): $10,000-$50,000
- Enterprise (cross-department, predictive analytics): $50,000+

**Ongoing Maintenance:**
- Basic monitoring: $500-$2,000/mo
- Active optimization: $2,000-$5,000/mo
- Full managed service: $5,000-$10,000+/mo

### Industry-Specific Build Fees (by niche)

| Niche | Typical Build Fee |
|---|---|
| Healthcare / med-spa | $8,000-$15,000 |
| Professional services | $5,000-$10,000 |
| Real estate | $3,000-$6,000 |
| EU AI compliance | $10,000-$50,000 |
| Manufacturing (custom) | $15,000-$75,000 |

### Value-Based Pricing (Growing Trend)

73% of AI consulting clients now prefer value-based pricing over hourly billing. Common structures:
- Base fee + 10-40% of documented cost savings
- Base fee + percentage of revenue lift attributable to AI
- Outcome-based with a fee floor (e.g., $5K/month minimum + 15% of savings above $50K/year)

### Recommended BGD Pricing Framework

Based on the competitive gap and market benchmarks, here's a pricing structure that would be competitive in the Des Moines mid-market:

**Tier 1: AI Discovery** | $2,500 one-time
- 2-3 hour on-site process audit
- Written report: top 3 automation opportunities ranked by ROI
- Implementation roadmap with timeline and cost estimates
- Designed to convert to Tier 2

**Tier 2: AI Build Sprint** | $7,500-$15,000 per automation
- 4-6 week implementation
- One working automation delivered and deployed
- Training for client team
- 30 days of post-launch support included

**Tier 3: AI Partnership** | $3,000-$5,000/month
- Ongoing monitoring and optimization of deployed systems
- Monthly strategy call
- 2 new automation improvements per quarter
- Priority support via Slack/email

**Value-Based Option:** For larger engagements, offer a base retainer ($2,500/mo) + 15% of documented annual savings. This aligns incentives and can significantly increase total revenue per client.
