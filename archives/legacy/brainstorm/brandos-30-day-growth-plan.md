# BrandOS 30-day growth plan

**Goal:** 10 active dealers on BrandOS + Terraplex Ag distributor = **$3,100/mo BrandOS total** by **2026-06-11**.

**Current:** 5 dealers ($1,100/mo, includes BK at $100 legacy) + Terraplex Ag ($600/mo) = $1,700/mo BrandOS.

**Net add:** +5 dealers at $250/mo each = **+$1,250/mo dealer-side**, +$1,400/mo total BrandOS (assuming distributor stays at $600).

See `decisions/log.md` 2026-05-11 entry for full goal lock + rationale.

---

## The frame: this is a sales motion, not a marketing campaign

In 30 days, "marketing" is too slow. The actionable moves are:

1. **Named prospect list with channel-mapping.** Who specifically, reached how.
2. **Pitch material ready.** One-pager or short deck for the dealer-tier offer.
3. **Onboarding pipeline tuned.** Already exists end-to-end (questionnaire → Bonsai contract → invoice). Verify it can absorb 5 new dealers without manual bottleneck.
4. **Weekly cadence with named touchpoints.** Four checkpoints across the 30 days.

Pure marketing (content, ads, social) is a 60-90+ day lever. Won't move this number.

## Positioning question to resolve before sending the HubSpot campaign

**Co-marketing frame vs. BGD-direct frame.** Sending BGD marketing through Terraplex's HubSpot to Terraplex's dealers raises a positioning choice:

- **Co-marketing with Terraplex** — message frames the BGD platform as a Terraplex-endorsed dealer offering. Implicit Cherity / Terraplex blessing. Higher trust, but ties BGD's positioning more tightly to Terraplex.
- **BGD-direct** — message presents Bearded Ginger Designs as a service provider, with Terraplex dealers as the target audience. Cleaner separation; lower implicit endorsement.

**Recommendation:** Confirm with Cherity before sending, even if it's a 30-second "I'm going to send something to dealers through HubSpot about the BGD platform — anything you want me to coordinate with you?" That protects the relationship that's already producing 3 of 5 of the current dealers. Lean co-marketing if she's onboard.
