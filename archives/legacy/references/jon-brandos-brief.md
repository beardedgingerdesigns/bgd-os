# BrandOS — Strategic Brief

*From Justin Lobaito · May 3, 2026*

---

## In a sentence

**BrandOS** is the productized name for the channel platform we've been building under Terraplex for the last two years. Operating system for multi-tier distribution networks: manufacturer to distributor to dealer.

## The framing

Two years building. Six dealers live. One distributor hub running. Now naming it forces a recognition: this isn't BGD tooling anymore. It's a product, and probably its own company over time. The Russell trip on Tuesday is the inflection point — adding the manufacturer tier on top makes BrandOS the three-tier system most B2B distribution networks actually run.

## What's already operating

- **One distributor hub** — Terraplex, two years live
- **Six dealer sites** — Pyro Ag, Black Knight, New Heights, Great River, Truss Services, Superior Drone
- **Schema-driven onboarding flow** — terraplex-onboarding.netlify.app — contract to live site in roughly one week
- **Manager app in production** — manager.beardedgingerdesigns.com
- **LLM-maintained knowledge wiki** — five scheduled research routines (curator, daily digest, monthly intelligence, quarterly deep-dive, weekly hub lint) running 24/7 on the spoke-hub repo
- **Six visual archetype themes** — no two dealer sites look the same

## What's still being built

- Hub-to-spoke content propagation automation (Phase 3, in active build)
- Multi-tenant client access (Phase 5, in active design)
- AI-driven dealer marketing material generation (in design)

## Who BrandOS targets

Vertical-agnostic platform. **Mid-market B2B manufacturers** with multi-tier distribution networks.

Not Caterpillar or John Deere. The next tier down. Privately-held, 5-50 distributors, 50-500 dealers, no in-house marketing-IT department. Currently underserved by enterprise SaaS like Zift ($50K+/year) and overserved by generic site builders like WordPress.

These are the same companies on Two Rivers' client list — Pioneer, DEVELON, Volvo CE, Genie, Miller Electric, Briggs & Stratton. Companies we already understand because we work with them.

## Where this is going (roadmap themes, ranked)

1. **Integrations layer** — CRM (HubSpot first), email marketing, calendar, analytics
2. **Down-channel communications + dealer portal** — message board, training delivery, asset access, action-required workflows
3. **Knowledge wiki provisioning per vertical** — replicate the terraplex-spoke-hub pattern programmatically when manufacturers in new verticals onboard
4. **Network analytics + insights** — cross-tier rollup; manufacturer sees regions, distributor sees their dealers, dealer sees themselves
5. **Compliance + governance** — regulatory change detection, audit logs, approval workflows

Plus internal substrate (database layer, white-labeling, observability) supporting everything above.

Deprioritized for now: self-service editing, AI-native customer surfaces, native commerce/billing, bulk-dealer-import workflows. They stay on the roadmap; just not in the immediate window.

## Where the existing relationship fits

Three places BrandOS connects to your existing work:

- **Terraplex is the founding distributor hub.** The architecture got proven with Terraplex's network. That's the credibility that lets a manufacturer like Russell take the platform thesis seriously.
- **LMG is the natural marketing-services partner.** As BrandOS scales to dealer-tier marketing services (video, trade-show packaging, brand work, ad placements), specialist partners plug into the platform as a layer. LMG fits that layer naturally given existing capabilities.
- **The anti-agency thesis aligns.** Curated specialists vs bundled overhead is structurally identical to BrandOS's productize-vs-custom thesis. Same operator instinct.

## What this isn't

- Not a financial pitch. No ask today.
- Not a contract conversation.
- Not an attempt to displace existing client relationships or change how Terraplex/LMG operate.

A strategic brief on what's been built, what it's becoming, and where existing relationships connect.
