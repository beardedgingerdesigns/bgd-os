## Strategic context worth knowing

### The Revolution / Terraplex authority shift

Adding Revolution as a manufacturer tier above Terraplex is a real change. Today, Terraplex is the hub. After Phase 2, Terraplex becomes a spoke (relative to Revolution) AND a hub (relative to dealers). The platform thesis assumes Revolution and Terraplex stay operationally aligned. That's the working assumption.

### The marketing-services layer

The leave-behind references "specialist marketing partners" in generic language for dealer-tier marketing services (video, trade-show packaging, brand work). Not naming any specific partner because the commercial structure isn't agreed yet. The architecture leaves the slot open for whoever fits.

### The network growth potential

Revolution has multiple potential distribution channels beyond Terraplex (military procurement, mapping integrators, international, OEM). The platform thesis is that each becomes a spoke under Revolution as the manufacturer hub. That's where the platform's economics get interesting.

## On the trip

Roughly $1,500 in travel cost. Tagging onto your trip — the logistics work because you're going for separate Terraplex business. The framing of the Revolution conversation is what determines whether the trip pays back.

Going in with the leave-behind, the platform thesis, and the founding-customer terms gives Russell something to react to that's bigger than a website project. That's the bet.
