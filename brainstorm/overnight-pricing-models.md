## 1. How Successful AI Service Companies Structure Their Offerings

- One straightforward workflow: $3,000-$10,000 project fee, $500-$1,500/mo retainer

- Real case study: 60-person marketing ops firm. Manual process costing $47K/yr. Built n8n workflow with LLM classification. $14K project fee + $900/mo retainer. Payback: 4.9 months.

**Morningside AI (AI Consulting + Implementation)**
- Runs 9-week discovery-to-roadmap engagements for operational efficiency
- Works with mid-market companies (Australian aged care, NZ industrial distributor)
- Strategy-first model: uncover inefficiencies, deliver roadmap, then build

**AGIX Technologies (US-Based AI Automation)**
- Three-tier model based on system autonomy:
  - Task-level automation (simple, rules-based)
  - Process-level automation (multi-step, some AI)
  - System-level automation (full agentic workflows)
- Won't take a project unless they can prove 3-5x return in the first year

**Fractional AI Officer / CTO Model**
- Monthly retainers: $5,000-$15,000/mo (50-70% less than a full-time CTO at $400K+/yr)
- AI/ML specialization commands 20-30% premium: $6,500-$16,000/mo
- Hourly: $150-$500/hr, with $200-$350 being the sweet spot for senior experts

**n8n Agency Partners**
- Free Automation Starter Audit as entry point
- $5,000 Automation Accelerator (production-ready workflows + workshops)
- Ongoing engagements from $10,000/mo

### The hybrid model wins

The most profitable AI agencies combine multiple revenue streams with a single client:
1. Initial consulting/strategy (builds relationship)
2. Custom implementation (high-value project)
3. Proprietary tool licensing (recurring revenue)
4. Ongoing optimization and support (retainer)

Most profitable services by margin:
- AI consulting: 80-90% margins
- Custom GPT/agent development: 90%+ margins
- Vertical SaaS: 70-80% margins

### Tier 1: AI Quickstart
**$750/mo + $2,500 setup fee**

*The entry point. Low risk for the client. Proves value fast.*

**What they get:**
- AI Readiness Audit (first month): Walk their top 5 workflows, identify automation candidates, deliver a prioritized roadmap

## 3. The Land-and-Expand Playbook

### The MindStudio "Ladder Framework" (proven model, May 2026)

Don't pitch retainers on the first call. Climb the ladder:

**Rung 0 -- The Door Opener ($500-$2,500)**
Get in the door with a low-risk engagement:
- AI Readiness Audit: $1,500 (structured review of workflows, tools, data)
- Process Mapping Session: $750 (half-day, map 3-5 processes, identify candidates)
- Quick-Win Build: $1,500 (one focused AI tool built in 1-2 days)

The price is low enough that most decision-makers approve without procurement. The scope is clear. You leave with a prioritized list of projects.

**End every Rung 0 with a findings presentation:**
1. What you found (specific processes, time costs, error rates)
2. What you'd build and why
3. A prioritized roadmap with rough costs
4. The expected ROI on the first two projects

Present three ideas, ranked by impact and ease. Make one an obvious quick win. Rung 1 gets closed in the room.

**Rung 1 -- First Paid Project ($3,000-$7,500)**
Build one automation. Prove it works. Document the ROI. Make the client a hero internally. This is the "look what we just did" moment.

**Rung 2 -- Second Project + Adjacent Win ($5,000-$10,000)**
Expand into a second department or workflow. The first project's success is your sales pitch.

**Rung 3 -- The Retainer Pitch**
"We've been doing one-off projects, but there's enough work here to make a monthly arrangement more efficient for both of us. Here's what that looks like."

By this point you've delivered 3-4 successful projects. The retainer is a natural next step.

### What triggers expansion

Based on real agency data, clients expand when:
- They see 10-20% improvement in the primary KPI from the pilot
- Results are measurable and repeatable across a second team
- A new department sees what the first one got and wants the same
- The client hits a seasonal crunch and needs more automation capacity
- New tools or AI capabilities enable things that weren't possible 6 months ago

### What makes clients sticky

- **Monitoring and maintenance.** AI systems drift. Models degrade. Data changes. Ongoing tuning isn't optional.
- **Quarterly optimization.** Show them what changed, what improved, what's next.
- **Institutional knowledge.** You know their systems, their data, their people. Switching cost is high.
- **Continuous quick wins.** Every month, ship something small that makes someone's day easier.

### The realistic expansion path

| Month | What happens | MRR from client |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Rung 0: AI Readiness Audit ($1,500 one-time) | $0 |
| 2-3 | Rung 1: First automation build ($5,000 project) | $0 |
| 4 | Client signs Tier 1 retainer | $750 |
| 6-8 | Second project success. Upgrade to Tier 2 | $2,500 |
| 10-12 | C-suite engagement. Upgrade to Tier 3 | $5,000 |

Conservative timeline: 10-12 months from first contact to Tier 3. Aggressive: 6 months.

Jon is a relationship salesman who works with Iowa businesses. He knows the owners, the GMs, the ops managers. He doesn't need to understand n8n or API integrations. He needs three things:

**1. The Problem Statement (what he says at lunch)**

**2. The One-Liner**
> "We're an AI operations partner. We find the manual work in your business and automate it. Fixed monthly price. You see ROI in 60 days or less."

**3. The Three-Card Menu**

Jon doesn't sell tiers. He sells a conversation:

| Card | What Jon Says | Price |
|---|---|---|
| **"The Test Drive"** | "Let us look at your top 5 processes for a day. We'll show you exactly what AI can do and what it'll save you. $1,500, and if you hate it, that's the end of it." | $1,500 one-time |

### What Jon does NOT need to explain

- How the AI works technically
- What tools or platforms are used
- How integrations are built
- API costs or token pricing

### What Jon DOES talk about

- **Hours saved.** "Your front desk person spends 3 hours a day on scheduling. We get that to 30 minutes."
- **Money recovered.** "You're losing $10K/mo in leads that go cold because nobody follows up fast enough."
- **Competitive pressure.** "Your competitors are already doing this. The ones who aren't are falling behind."
- **Risk reversal.** "Start with the audit. If we can't find $2,000/mo in savings, we'll tell you that."

### Jon's commission structure (suggested)

- 15-20% of first-year revenue on new accounts he brings
- 10% on upsells/expansions he facilitates
- Paid monthly as the retainer is collected (not upfront)
- Example: Jon lands a Tier 2 client at $2,500/mo = $30K/yr = $4,500-$6,000 to Jon in year one

## 6. Scaling Without Scaling Your Time

### What keeps margins high

- **Build once, deploy many.** The email triage automation you build for Client A is 80% reusable for Client B. Template your solutions.
- **AI builds AI.** Use Claude Code, n8n, Make.com to build client solutions. Your tooling makes you 3-5x faster than a traditional consultancy.
- **Monitoring is cheap.** Once deployed, most automations need <1 hr/mo of monitoring. Charge for the value, not the time.
- **Productize the patterns.** After 5-10 clients, you'll see the same 10 workflows over and over. Package them as turnkey solutions.

### The productized solution library (build as you go)

Every client engagement should produce a reusable template:

| Solution | Typical Client Need | Build Once, Deploy Many |
|---|---|---|
| Email triage + auto-routing | Every business | Yes |
| Lead qualification + CRM routing | Sales teams | Yes |
| Appointment scheduling automation | Service businesses | Yes |
| Invoice processing + data extraction | Finance teams | Yes |
| Customer support FAQ bot | Customer-facing teams | Yes |
| Report generation + dashboarding | Management | Yes |
| Employee onboarding workflow | HR teams | Yes |
| Content drafting + review pipeline | Marketing teams | Yes |

### When to hire (not yet)

Don't hire until you're consistently at $15K+/mo MRR and turning away work. When you do:
- First hire: Junior automation builder ($50-60K). Handles Tier 1 maintenance and simple builds.
- Second hire: Senior AI engineer ($80-100K). Handles Tier 2/3 custom work.
- Jon stays on commission. Never goes salary until revenue supports it.
